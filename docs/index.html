EIP-8038 Repricing Analysis

EIP-8038 reprices a set of EVM state-access operations — cold and warm storage and account access, plus account and storage writes. This site reports an empirical re-derivation of those gas costs: NNLS regressions fit on client benchmark runtimes, anchored at 100 Mgas/s, with the worst-case client selected per gas parameter. The methodology page documents the full pipeline.

Of the 9 gas parameters analysed, 9 warrant an increase and 0 could be reduced relative to the current cost table. The pressure is concentrated in the state-access operations that EIP-8038 targets — cold storage and account access and the account and storage writes that run below the 100 Mgas/s anchor; see the new-gas proposal for the per-parameter breakdown.

Preliminary results. These gas parameters are not yet final: the underlying measurements are still being refined as the execution clients continue to optimize their state-access paths. As those optimizations land we expect the per-client runtimes to converge, which may shift the worst-case selection and the resulting gas parameters.
